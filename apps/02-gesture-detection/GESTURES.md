# Gesture Vocabulary and Interaction Mapping

This document outlines the systematic mapping of core interaction patterns onto physical gestures for both near-range (finger/hand level) and far-range (arm/body level) scenarios. The data and reliability constraints define the logical fences and time windows required to validate the raw coordinate streams based on the benchmarks in `OBSERVATIONS.md`.

## 2. Selection for Prototyping

To evaluate different architectural patterns of signal processing, three gestures (split into two tactical categories) have been selected for the functional prototype:

### Gesture A: "Next" & "Back" (Swipe/Point Right & Left) - _Category: Deterministic & High-Stability_

- **Rationale:** These directional gestures heavily rely on Landmark 8 (Index Finger Tip), which proved to be the most stable coordinate in our benchmark pipeline. They serve as the baseline for evaluating geometric thresholding and mirror-image logic without complex filtering.
- **Primary Engineering Challenge:** Implementing a robust spatial fence to reliably differentiate a deliberate directional pointing gesture from accidental hand lifting or resting postures.

### Gesture B: "Start gesture control" (Activation Trigger via Pinky-Pinch) - _Category: Complex & Noise-Prone_

- **Rationale:** This gesture deliberately forces the "Phantom-Point" effect documented in [`OBSERVATIONS.md`](../01-poc-jitter/OBSERVATIONS.md). Because the model switches from optical detection to anatomical probability when the thumb touches the pinky, the incoming data is highly volatile (jitter spikes to **0.022**).
- **Primary Engineering Challenge:** Proving that a reliable binary state change (On/Off) can be filtered out of highly unstable raw data without causing stutter or false activations.
